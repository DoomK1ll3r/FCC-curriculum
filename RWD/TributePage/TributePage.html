Richard Feynman

Who?

Richard Feynman was an extraordinary physicist, very well known around the world.

His work involved quantum mechanics, quantum electrodynamics, particle physics, nanotechnology etc. Making him one of the greatest physicist of the 20 century, and one of the greatest of all the time.

Contributions

  • (1943) Manhattan Project: He was assigned to Hans Bethe's Theorical Division, beign a group leader. He and Bethe develped the Bethe-Feynman formula for calculatin the yield of a fission bomb.

  • (1948) Feynman Diagrams: He used for the first time his concept of Feynman Diagrams.

  • (1949) Quantum Electrodynamics: Developed his own version of the Quantum Electrodynamics Theory.

  • (1950) Path Integral Formulation: He made a description of quantum mechanics that generalizes the stationay action principle of classical mechanics

  • (1963) Feynman Lectures on Physics: As a teacher, he made a compilation of lectures about undergraduate physics.

  • (1969) Parton Model: He proposed a model useful to undestand the behavior of patricles within hadrons.

  • (1970) Chromodynamics: A theory that describes the strong interaction between gluons and quarks.

  • (1982) Feynman Lectures on Computation: A compilation of lectures that involved classical computation and Quantum Computation

Personal Timeline

  • (May 11, 1918) Birth: Richard Feynman is borns in New York City

  • (1935) MIT: He enrolls at the MIT, studying electrical engineering.

  • (1939) Princeton: Starts graduate studies at Princeton.

  • (1945) Ph.D.: Gets his Ph.D. in theorical physics at Princeton.

  • (1947) Cornell University: Joins as an associate professor at Cornell

  • (1950) Caltech: Joins at Caltech as a full time professor.

  • (1965) Nobel: Gets the Nobel Prize in physics.

  • (1979) "Surely You're Joking, Mr.Feynman": Writes an autobiography full of anecdotes.

  • (February 15, 1986) Death: Dies in Los angeles, after a cancer.
